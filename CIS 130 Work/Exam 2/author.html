Marquis De Sade

A rather infamous and controversial figure, Marquis De Sade was a prolific writer who spent much of his life in between various prisons and asylums due to being particularly libertine in his way of living, acts of blasphemy during a period where theocratic leanings were still strong in much of Europe, his bisexuality during a time where sodomy was punishable by death (for which he was sentenced in absentia though he avoided execution), and the various scandals. Despite his notoriety he remained a prolific writer and philosopher whose stark refusal to abide by societal norms of the time, and revolutionary stances proved impactful on a number of notable figures and to a lesser degree on societal shifts as a whole. The nature of his controvesial life, the burning of much of his unpublished works, the abundance of enemies made during his life, and the period in which he lived together make certain aspects of his life hazy.

Dying Man

A libertine and skeptic within the dialogue, within the dialogue they are presented upon their deathbed discussing their circumstance and regrets with the Priest, while also heavily dealing with their thoughts on morality, existence, and religion in general.

Priest

A priest who has come to offer last rites to a man on his deathbed. Given the Marquis De Sade's own Jesuit schooling, the prevalence of catholocism in general, and the specifics of the discussion it would seem that the priest similarly is intended to be a Jesuit Priest.
